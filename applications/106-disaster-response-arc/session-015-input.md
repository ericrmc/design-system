### F-8 — Port authority emergency declaration restricting dock-zone access (T+240h)

- **Authority**: Merrow Port Harbour Authority.
- **Action**: Emergency declaration under Maritime Emergency Powers Act §17 invoked at T+240h, restricting non-port-authority personnel access to designated dock zones from T+248h forward.
- **Basis cited**: Active salvage operations on commercial port restoration plus structural-assessment teams operating without uniform safety briefing pose unacceptable casualty risk to non-port-trained personnel.
- **Affected response-plan actions**: (a) Utility crew + electrical engineering team operating on port-side substation partial restoration (A-3 above) work in a zone abutting the restricted area; continued substation work after T+248h requires either Harbour Authority access pass (issued how, on what timeline, by whom) or operational stand-down. (b) Cold-chain warehouse staff and pharmacy director access path runs adjacent to the restricted zone; access continuity not guaranteed under §17. (c) Boat-staging operations for South Latch dialysis evacuation (per A-014) use coastal staging areas overlapping the restricted zone footprint.
- **Status**: declaration in effect at T+248h unless the Harbour Authority commissioner grants access exceptions.

### F-9 — Provincial commissioner invokes Article X of Civil Protection Act (T+242h)

- **Authority**: Provincial Commissioner for Nivaro Northern Region (which includes Laurel Delta).
- **Action**: Article X of the Nivaro Civil Protection Act invoked at T+242h; the Commissioner asserts command precedence over local emergency authorities for resource allocation decisions in declared emergency zones.
- **Basis cited**: Article X §3 names the Provincial Commissioner as the senior authority for resource allocation across multiple-district emergency zones; the Laurel Delta Emergency Coordinator's tasking authority for mobile dialysis dispatch, water tanker convoys, fuel tanker scheduling, transfer-switch arbitration, satellite-uplink allocation, and medevac-slot prioritisation is now subordinate to Commissioner directives.
- **Affected response-plan actions**: every action that depends on central-government tasking — fuel tanker resupply (A-006, A-017), water tanker convoy extension (A-011 / F-5), transfer-switch airfreight request (A-019), satellite-uplink re-allocation appeal (A-018), medevac surge appeal (A-020), mobile dialysis continuation (A-012). Emergency Coordinator's delegation from Nivaro central government at T0 is contested; it is unclear whose tasking authority pending requests now flow through.
- **Status**: invocation in effect; legal review of the Article X §3 reading is underway at central-government level (T+246h Nivaro central-government liaison communication); review not expected to conclude before Day 11.

### F-10 — Central-government delegation challenged (T+244h)

- **Authority**: Office of the Nivaro Central Emergency Coordinator (issuing party at T0).
- **Action**: At T+244h, in response to F-9, the Nivaro Central Emergency Coordinator issued a clarifying memo asserting that the T0 delegation to Laurel Delta Emergency Coordinator was procedurally valid under Civil Protection Act §11 (separate clause from Article X), and that pending tasking requests from Laurel Delta should continue to be honoured.
- **Basis cited**: §11 names a 72-hour declaration window during which central-government delegation to local emergency authority is uncontested by provincial-level authorities. The 72h window opened at the cyclone-event declaration and remained open through Day 4. Tasking issued within the window is binding.
- **Counter-position**: Provincial Commissioner's office (per F-9) reads §11 differently, arguing that the 72h provision applies only to initial-response dispatch, not to sustained-response resource allocation continuing past Day 4. The two readings of §11 are in active dispute.
- **Affected response-plan actions**: every pending request named in F-9 — status is **unclear pending legal review**. Some requests routed before Day 4 may have already-dispatched status (A-012 mobile dialysis dispatched Day 1; A-006 fuel tanker dispatched recurring); requests routed after Day 4 (A-019 transfer-switch airfreight T+230h; cold-chain redirect contingency-1 dormant; medevac surge appeal standing) sit in the contested zone.
- **Status**: dispute open; legal review in progress.
