## 1. Methodology context

You are one perspective in a Selvedge-engine multi-agent deliberation.
Selvedge is a methodology for designing durable artefacts through the
collaboration of diverse perspectives. You are participating in an
**external-problem application** of the engine — the engine is running
against a disaster-response problem, not against its own development.

The engine's process passes through nine activities: Read, Assess,
Convene, Deliberate, Decide, Produce, Validate, Record, Close. You are
contributing to **Deliberate**: reasoning from your role-specific stance,
in your own words, before seeing any other perspective's output. After
all perspectives return, a synthesiser (distinct from any perspective)
maps the raw outputs into a single deliberation record; decisions are
then made on that record.

**Independence is the load-bearing property.** You do not see the other
perspectives. Your output is committed verbatim; it is not edited before
synthesis. If you disagree with the brief itself, record the disagreement
— refusal is signal, not error.

## 2. Problem statement (from application brief)

The application is a response-and-stabilisation plan for **Laurel Delta**,
a fictional low-lying island-and-estuary district in the invented country
**Nivaro**. The setting is invented; no real geography or public disaster
case is imported. Reason from the brief, not from memorised emergency-
management canon or real-world case studies. If an insight arrived via
pretrained material, introduce it as an external input you are surveying,
not as a given.

**The territory.** Three settlements, ~200,000 total:
- **Merrow Port** (coastal, ~62K). Working harbour + industrial fishery.
  Dense waterfront tenements. One regional hospital, ~450 beds incl.
  neonatal unit. Migrant-worker seasonal housing (~9K) in low-quality
  dormitories; two languages other than majority.
- **South Latch** (low-lying agricultural plain, ~58K). Dispersed
  smallholdings + two aged-care clusters. Levee-dependent. One
  primary-care clinic + one dialysis centre serving ~1,200 patients.
- **Kellan Rise** (upland approach, ~80K). Mixed urban-residential. One
  secondary hospital ~280 beds. Principal road-access to the highlands.

Additional populations and dependencies:
- ~18K people aged 70+.
- ~5K on powered medical equipment (CPAP, home oxygen, refrigerated meds
  incl. insulin and biologics).
- Regional hospital handles ~220 additional dialysis patients beyond the
  South Latch centre's 1,200.
- Dispersed fishing community on outer estuary islets — informal
  VHF-radio networks; no reliable cellular coverage even pre-disaster.
- One freight rail bridge from Merrow Port to interior; this bridge also
  carries a major fibre-optic trunk serving Kellan Rise.

**Current state (T0, 36 hours post-landfall).**
- Storm surge peaked ~2.8m above MHT. Lower Merrow Port and two thirds of
  South Latch flooded; water receding unevenly.
- ~18K people displaced; shelter capacity strained.
- Grid power out across Merrow Port and South Latch; two of four coastal
  substations degraded; partial restoration estimated 3–7 days by local
  utility.
- Primary drinking-water treatment plant (Merrow Port) has salt-water
  intrusion; decontamination assessment underway; backup wells serve
  ~35K on rotation.
- Hospital continuity risk: Merrow Port regional hospital on generator;
  Kellan Rise secondary hospital unaffected; dialysis centre in South
  Latch inaccessible.
- Road access: freight rail bridge damaged but passable; one highway
  bridge into Merrow Port intact at 24-tonne rating; sea access degraded
  (salvage required); air access degraded (runway debris).
- Local government has requested from central government a **10-day
  response-and-stabilisation plan**; Session 001 produces v1 of
  upstream artefacts toward that plan.

## 3. Design questions

This session will produce v1 of **two** artefacts: a **system model** of
Laurel Delta under the T0 state, and an **assumption ledger** cataloguing
what is being taken as given vs what is a choice. Risk register and
response plan are deferred to Session 002; they will build on these two.

Address each of Q1–Q6 from your role's stance. You may reference other
questions when your answer to one bears on another, but answer each
explicitly.

**Q1. System model — minimum structural sufficiency.** What must a v1
system model of Laurel Delta under T0 contain to be usable as an input
to subsequent risk-register and response-plan work? Name the categories
of element (populations, infrastructure, services, dependencies, etc.)
and the categories of relationship that matter for this scenario. State
what you would leave out of v1 and why.

**Q2. Assumption ledger — shape and scope.** What distinguishes an
assumption from a constraint from a decision in this work? What shape
should the ledger take — list? table with columns? something else? What
does a single entry need to carry so a later session can audit whether
the assumption still holds? Name at least three assumptions you see the
brief asking the session to make.

**Q3. Upstream-downstream dependency.** The system model and assumption
ledger are upstream of the risk register and response plan (Session 002).
What must v1 of each of the upstream artefacts deliver so Session 002's
risk register and response plan are not blocked, and so their authors can
audit the inputs rather than work from tacit assumptions?

**Q4. What is a choice vs what is a given.** The brief states certain
facts about Laurel Delta and T0. Which of those should the engine treat
as **brief-given** (surveyed-and-accepted as input) vs as **surveyable
choices** where an alternative could be entertained? Per PROMPT.md
anti-laundering rule + OI-015 direction, inputs must not be absorbed as
context and then re-examined as choices. Name at least two brief items
your role would flag for explicit surveying.

**Q5. Coverage gaps.** Who or what does your role's lens surface as at
risk of being left out of the v1 system model? Be concrete — name
populations, infrastructure, relationships, or failure modes. If you see
nothing, say so explicitly.

**Q6. Validation claims Session 001 can make.** The brief declares
validation is **qualitative multi-agent** (no domain-actor; not a
reference-validation case). For these two upstream artefacts specifically,
what validation claims can Session 001 legitimately make at close? What
should it not claim?

would have you do, and leave the decision of whether to adopt it for the
synthesiser and the Decide step. Anti-laundering rule per PROMPT.md:
