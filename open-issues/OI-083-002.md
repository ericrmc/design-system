# Worked rubric for the coding review loop's severity taxonomy

Medium/low boundary of the coding-review four-level severity taxonomy is ambiguous; needs calibrated rubric with worked examples.

The S083 reviewer pass surfaced ambiguity at the medium/low boundary of the four-level severity taxonomy (critical/high/medium/low) introduced in specifications/methodology.md v3 §When to review §Coding review loop. Two reviewers reading the same finding could classify it differently — in particular, a missing test assertion (medium per spec: correctness/safety/coverage gap) versus a clarity nit (low). The methodology spec records the deferral and notes that genuinely-borderline findings are treated as medium for the loop's termination condition until calibrated. Why it matters: severity is the gating field for the review loop's termination — medium-or-higher must be addressed before close; low can be deferred. If the boundary is ambiguous, reviewers can game termination by classifying findings as low, or stall progress by classifying them as medium. What's needed: a worked rubric with at least three pairs of medium/low examples drawn from real reviewer output, plus a tie-breaker rule. Deferred to second or third coding session that produces enough reviewer findings to anchor the rubric in observed practice.
