# decision_supports / alternative_rejections allow NULL cited_object_id on cite-required bases

13 supports + 5 alternatives + 6 rejections in substrate; only 2 carry a non-NULL cited_object_id; cite-requiring bases land without cites.

decision_supports.cited_object_id and alternative_rejections.cited_object_id are nullable. Across the 13 supports + 5 alternatives + 6 rejections currently in the substrate, only 2 rows carry a non-NULL cited_object_id. Bases that semantically require a cite — prior_decision, review_finding, deliberation, spec_clause, engine_feedback, operator_override — are landing without one, defeating the traceability promise. The S082 P-1 schema sketch named alternative_rejections.cited_object_id fk not null. The substrate landed it nullable to admit inferior_tradeoff and too_large_for_session rejections, which can be argued from session-internal reasoning without a prior-object cite. That carve-out swallowed the constraint. What's needed: a per-basis constraint table (cite_required: true/false per basis enum), or a CHECK constraint that pairs basis with cited_object_id. Implementation: enumerate cite-requiring bases and refuse NULL cited_object_id at insert time; admit the carve-out for inferior_tradeoff and too_large_for_session explicitly. Coordinate with OI-086-001/-002 broader spec-write-path tightening.
