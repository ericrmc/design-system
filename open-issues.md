### OI-017: Engine-vs-methodology reframing — separability of engine-definition from development-provenance; distinct execution prompt for external applications
**Surfaced:** Session 016 (per D-073; sourced from operator session-open input preserved verbatim at `provenance/016-operator-reframing-assessment/00-operator-input.md`)
**Status:** Open — activation trigger specified; preferred-starting-point hypothesis frame recorded

The operator's Session 016 session-open note proposed a structural reframing: the workspace may be better understood as an **engine** (defined inputs, procedures, outputs) rather than as a **methodology** in the abstract. Under the reframing, every session 001–015 has been a self-development *application* of the engine; external applications would apply the same engine definition to non-self problems using the same kernel, multi-agent-deliberation, validation, and identity specifications. Two structural consequences were offered as input-rather-than-direction:

1. **Engine definition may benefit from being distinguishable from development provenance** so that other applications can load the engine without loading the development history.
2. **Different applications may require different execution prompts** — PROMPT.md currently drives self-development; an external-problem application may need a distinct prompt that loads the engine definition plus application-specific context.

Session 016 performed the PROMPT.md-mandated surveying step (`00-assessment.md` §3) and hypothesising step (§4), producing three competing hypotheses:

- **H1 (no reframing):** retain current framing; record the observation as clarifying prose without specification changes. Minimal revision cost; premature-naming risk low; but latent ambiguity remains.
- **H2 (full reframing):** split PROMPT.md into engine-prompt + development-prompt; add engine-definition manifest; rename or retitle `methodology-kernel.md` as `engine-kernel.md`; update `workspace-structure.md` to distinguish engine / development / application file classes; update `identity.md` to say Selvedge names the engine; specify an application-initialisation pattern. High revision cost; speculative-design risk for applications that have not yet occurred; kernel rename may trigger unnecessary version-chain work.
- **H3 (partial reframing):** name the distinction via a new `engine-manifest.md` specification; minor updates to `identity.md` and PROMPT.md acknowledging the two application kinds; no split of PROMPT.md; no rename of existing files. Medium revision cost; addresses observation (1) but not (2); lets the first actual external application drive the rest of the restructure.
