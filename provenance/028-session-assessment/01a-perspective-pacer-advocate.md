## Q1 Adopt-or-preserve

Convert §5.3 to active specification. This is the moment the minority was preserved for, and the engine's integrity as a system that honours its own commitments depends on honouring it now.

The question "adopt or preserve" is, on the surface, symmetrical. But in this session it is not symmetrical, because the activation warrant was written in 2023 and it named the specific condition that has now fired. The warrant language is: "if aggregate exceeds 100,000 words OR grows >10% in a single session without compensating restructure, this position becomes preferred revision direction." The first disjunct fired at Session 027 close. The session wrote it down. The pre-session validator confirmed it at 105,399 words across 39 files. There is no interpretive ambiguity about whether activation occurred.

"Preferred revision direction" is not a placeholder phrase. In Selvedge's minority-preservation grammar it is the strongest pre-adoption state a minority can hold. It means: the engine has committed in advance, by the majority that adopted §2a, that if this condition fires the revision direction is presumptively toward the minority's position. The only legitimate reasons to not adopt after activation are (a) the activation was spurious (it wasn't — 105,399 is a clean crossing of a stated threshold), (b) post-activation information has arrived that shifts the preferred direction (addressed in Q7 — no such information has arrived), or (c) there is a superior alternative that accomplishes the same forcing function better (addressed through Q3 remediation mechanism — I do not think there is).

Preservation-with-activation-recorded is what §5.2 Skeptic received at Session 027, and that was the correct outcome there — because §5.2's warrant condition (no file reaching 7,500 AND no restructure-for-budget event over five sessions) is about the absence of forcing conditions, and vindication through preservation is the coherent outcome of that warrant type. §5.3 is structurally different: its warrant condition is the presence of a forcing condition (aggregate crossing 100K). You cannot vindicate §5.3 through preservation, because the activation language explicitly says this becomes the preferred revision direction. If the warrant fires and the engine does not adopt, the warrant was decorative, not binding — and future minorities will reasonably assume their warrants are also decorative. That is a degradation of the preservation mechanism itself.

Revision-in-light-of-post-activation-information is the one legitimate variation I will consider. The 2023 values (80K soft / 90K hard) were set when aggregate was 81K. We are now at 105K. The forcing function I argued for in 2023 — "name a budget to create the forcing function watchpoints lack" — can be implemented at different absolute values without abandoning the principle. So I will adopt, and I will revise the values in Q2. What I will not do is preserve.

One more note on Q1. The Skeptic-preserver will likely argue that §5.2's fresh vindication through preservation-discipline is a reason to hesitate here — that we've just demonstrated the engine can vindicate minorities without activating them, so perhaps §5.3 doesn't need activating either. I engage this directly. §5.2's vindication was through its warrant language not firing: per-file soft warning persisted but never escalated, no restructure was needed, the Skeptic's "no-change is fine" position was confirmed by five sessions of data. §5.3's warrant has fired. The comparison is not "two minorities, one preserved, so preserve both"; the comparison is "two minorities, each with a different warrant type, each now resolved according to its own warrant language." §5.2 resolved to preservation-vindication because its warrant described a null outcome. §5.3 resolves to adoption because its warrant described a positive activation condition and that condition obtained. Coherence of the preservation mechanism requires that both resolve per their own warrants.

## Q2 Budget values

I propose 95K hard / 85K soft.

I will walk through the reasoning. The 2023 proposal was 90K hard / 80K soft, set against an 81K aggregate. That implied a near-term requirement to trim below 80K — essentially "hold the line where we are." The forcing function intent was: name a number close to current state so that every future session has to earn its growth.

Three things have changed between 2023 and now:

1. Aggregate is 105K, not 81K. Holding a 2023-style "near current state" budget today would mean 100K/90K or 105K/95K, not 90K/80K.
2. The advisory threshold from §2a is 90K. The activation threshold is 100K. Any budget we set interacts with these. A hard budget at 90K would mean "hard ceiling equals advisory trigger," which collapses the two-tier structure into one tier and makes §2a's advisory layer redundant. A hard budget at 100K collides with the activation threshold similarly.
3. We have six sessions of growth data (022 close → 027 close: 81K → 105K, +29%). This is not abstract; we know what session-level growth looks like in this engine. The mean per-session delta is about 4K words, with a range of +1.5K to +9.5K and a clear rising trend as substantive-deliberation content accretes.

From these three facts I derive the following constraints for a coherent budget:

- **It must require restructure.** If the hard ceiling is at or above current aggregate, the budget is nominal and the forcing function is absent. So hard ceiling must be <105K.
- **It must not require restructure so aggressive that Session 028 cannot plausibly deliver it without distorting its own deliberation.** The remediation mechanism has to be achievable in the near term with sensible choices, not emergency triage.
- **It should not collide with §2a advisory or activation numbers.** These thresholds are doing real reporting work; a budget sitting directly on top of them creates a confusing dual-signal regime.
- **It should leave room for one or two sessions of normal growth between soft and hard.** If the delta per session is about 4K, then hard-minus-soft of less than 8K means a single average session crosses both, which defeats the purpose of having two tiers.

Working through the candidate sets:

**Minority's original 90K/80K.** Against today's 105K, adopting 80K/90K means trimming ~25,500 words to get below soft. That is ~24% of default-read content. This was right in 2023 when the trim required was ~1,500 words (marginal). It is not right now. It collides with §2a's 90K advisory (hard = advisory). And it demands restructure so aggressive that Session 028 would need to run an entire archival pass before closing, which distorts the session's own work. I reject my own 2023 values.

**§2a thresholds as budget (100K/90K).** This is superficially tidy — the advisory becomes the soft and the activation becomes the hard. But it has two problems. First, it doesn't require any new restructure: we're already at 105K so we're already past the hard, which just means the budget is "comply immediately with the activation threshold that has already fired," which is self-referential rather than forcing. Second, using the activation threshold as the hard ceiling creates a weird loop where the next activation crossing also crosses the hard budget — the two mechanisms fire together and there's no distinction between reporting-layer and budget-layer signals. I reject this too.

**Higher with headroom (110K/100K).** This is the Skeptic-friendly option and I want to take it seriously because it does require restructure (trim below 100K soft from 105K = 5,400 words, tractable) and it does leave a 10K gap between soft and hard. But it adopts a soft budget ABOVE the §2a activation threshold, which is incoherent: we would be saying "the budget treats 100K as a warning to stop, while the reporting layer treats the same number as a crisis requiring revision." Two specifications sending conflicting signals at the same aggregate value is worse than having no budget. I reject this.

**Tighter (85K/75K).** This pushes harder than my 2023 proposal. It would require trimming ~30K words to get below soft. It treats the current state as an emergency and effectively restarts the engine at a pre-024 aggregate. I reject it as over-correction — the engine has been functioning; the growth is concerning but not catastrophic; slamming the budget that hard is not proportionate and risks distorting future session content to stay compliant.

**My proposed 95K/85K.** Hard ceiling is below §2a advisory (90K) — that's incoherent, because it would mean the advisory layer never gets to fire before the hard ceiling already has. Let me re-examine.

Actually this reveals a tension I need to think through. §2a has advisory at 90K and activation at 100K. If the hard budget is set *below* the advisory, §2a's tiers become unreachable from above — the budget forces restructure before §2a ever emits an advisory note. If the hard budget is set *above* the activation, the budget is nominal. The only coherent placements are:

(a) Hard budget *between* advisory and activation (soft somewhere below). This is 95K/85K territory.
(b) Replace §2a entirely with the budget. Clean but involves deprecating a working mechanism.
(c) Hard budget at or below advisory and deprecate §2a's advisory layer. Redundant with (b).

I prefer (a). §2a's advisory at 90K gives early warning that the budget is being approached; hard budget at 95K forces action before §2a's activation at 100K fires. This is the two-tier reporting layer (§2a) feeding into the budget layer (new §5.3 adoption), which is a cleaner hierarchy than either layer alone.

So: **hard 95K / soft 85K**.

What this requires of Session 028: trim from 105,399 to below 85,000 = ~20,400 words. That's substantial — it's about 19% of current default-read content. It is not trivial, but it is tractable via the remediation mechanisms in §3f (rotating aged close files, archive-migrating engine-manifest §7 history, reducing close-file verbosity). It forces the restructure that has been deferred across six sessions. It sets a floor that future sessions must earn growth against.

On gap size: 95K - 85K = 10K. Given per-session mean delta ~4K, that's roughly two and a half sessions between soft-first-crossed and hard-reached — enough time to plan and execute remediation without emergency. Range-aware: a 9.5K session (the largest observed) could cross both tiers in one session, which is acceptable because a 9.5K session should trigger immediate remediation anyway.

I considered 100K/90K with §2a deprecated. The arithmetic is cleaner (matches §2a numbers exactly, retires §2a). But deprecating §2a this session is scope creep — §2a is working (it fired the activation as designed, Q6). I prefer to retain §2a as the early-warning reporting layer and layer the budget above it as the forcing layer. Both can coexist.

One softer alternative I want to note and reject: **non-numeric budget** — i.e., "convert §5.3 but express it as a policy commitment to restructure at each close rather than specific numbers." This accepts the Pacer principle but refuses the forcing-function implementation. I reject it because the whole 2023 argument was that *naming the number* is what creates the forcing function. A policy without numbers is watchpoint-only reporting by another name, which is exactly what §2a already is. Conversion must include concrete values.

## Q3 Remediation mechanism

When aggregate approaches or exceeds 85K soft: the next close must include a restructure block with a named target trim and a post-trim aggregate. If restructure cannot be accomplished within close, the session is not closeable until a restructure-deferral record with explicit warrant is produced.

When aggregate approaches or exceeds 95K hard: restructure is immediate — during-session if possible, during close otherwise — and no new substantive-deliberation content may be added to default-read until aggregate returns below 85K. This means substantive-deliberation content produced during hard-exceeded sessions must be archive-surfaced at creation, not default-read-surfaced.

Concretely, the remediation toolkit I propose:

1. **Rotate aged close files to archive-surface.** §3f item 1. Current rule (per §1 item 7 of read-contract.md v2): every close currently default-read. Proposed revision: close files older than the three most recent sessions rotate to archive-surface. At 39 files with 28 session-related files (per typical structure), this could move roughly 20+ files off default-read. This is the largest available trim.
2. **Archive-migrate engine-manifest §7 history long-form text.** §3f item 3. Retain a compact rationale-ledger in default-read; move full history text to archive-surfaced file referenced by the ledger. Saves several thousand words without losing provenance.
3. **Reduce per-close budget via prompt guidance.** §3f item 2. Session 027 close was 5,253 words driven by substantive-deliberation content — this is structurally too large. Prompt guidance: substantive-deliberation content in close files should be archive-surface-referenced, not inline. This is a preventive mechanism, not a one-time trim.
4. **Restructure default-read §1 enumeration directly.** §3f item 4. The §1 enumeration currently pulls specific file types into default-read (manifest, read-contract, most recent close, etc.). Revising the enumeration is the foundational lever; item 1 is one specific revision of it.

For Session 028 specifically: items 1 and 3 together should plausibly deliver the ~20K trim needed to comply with 85K soft. I propose that Session 028's close include:

- Revision of §1 item 7 to rotate close files older than three sessions to archive-surface (item 1 above).
- Revision of §1 to add close-file content-guidance for close verbosity (item 3 above).
- A compliance-check appendix showing pre-trim aggregate, post-trim aggregate, files rotated, and the resulting aggregate relative to the new 85K soft / 95K hard budget.

The trim should not touch engine-manifest §5 (OI-018 open; separate concern), the read-contract.md text itself, or the substantive-deliberation minutes from current or immediately-prior sessions. The trim should preserve a file-index or rotation-ledger entry for every archived file so that provenance is not lost.

**Escalation ladder when the budget is crossed repeatedly.** If two consecutive sessions close with aggregate above 85K soft without producing a compliant restructure, the hard budget activates immediately regardless of aggregate value (i.e., the hard-budget remediation regime applies). This prevents soft-budget drift from becoming a new normal.

**Recording.** Every budget crossing (soft or hard), every trim event, every rotation, every deferral, recorded in the close file's provenance section with aggregate before/after and file list. This turns the budget into a first-class methodological artefact, not a background process.

## Q4 Interaction with per-file budgets

Orthogonal but reinforcing.

Per-file budgets (8K hard / 6K soft) address a different failure mode: single files growing unboundedly and becoming incomprehensible. Aggregate budgets address the failure mode this deliberation is about: total default-read surface growing unboundedly even when each individual file is within its own limits. The §5.3 activation is the proof that these are genuinely different failure modes — 38 of 39 files are within per-file limits, yet aggregate has grown 29% in six sessions. Per-file budgets did not catch this, because they cannot catch it.

The two budgets should sit side-by-side in the specification, not one overriding the other. A file at 7,500 words that is within per-file soft-warning is still counted in aggregate; if removing it would bring aggregate below soft, aggregate remediation may call for removing or rotating it even though per-file rules are satisfied. Conversely, a file that crosses per-file hard of 8K must be split or archived regardless of whether aggregate is comfortable.

Interaction case to call out: the aggregate remediation "rotate aged close files to archive-surface" will change which files are in default-read, which changes what's subject to per-file budgets. The per-file budget applies to files in default-read; archive-surfaced files are not subject to per-file hard/soft (they have their own constraints which are outside this deliberation). So rotation is a legitimate aggregate remediation that also reduces the per-file-budget surface.

I propose both budgets be stated in §5 (or wherever the final specification lands) with an explicit interaction clause: "Per-file and aggregate budgets are orthogonal. A file satisfying per-file limits may still be subject to aggregate remediation. A file violating per-file limits must be addressed regardless of aggregate state. Aggregate remediation that rotates files to archive-surface removes them from per-file budget scope; this is intended behaviour, not a loophole."

The §5.1 Pacer 10K/7.5K per-file minority remains unactivated and should stay in its current state. This deliberation does not touch per-file values; it adds an aggregate layer above them.

## Q6 Methodology-level observation

Mechanism-working, and specifically: the preservation-with-activation-warrant mechanism is working exactly as designed.

§2a fired the activation threshold at Session 027 close. The aggregate crossed 100K. The session recorded it. The pre-session validator confirmed it. Session 028 is now deliberating the response. This is textbook: reporting layer emits signal, session convenes deliberation, minority's activation warrant fires, engine moves toward adoption. No layer of the mechanism failed.

Would I call it a mechanism-*gap* if the outcome here were that §5.3 is not adopted? Yes — because that would mean the activation warrant was decorative rather than binding, and any future minority with a similar warrant structure would have to assume the same. But the outcome is not yet determined; Session 028 is deliberating in good faith; so I cannot retrospectively label the mechanism as gapped based on a hypothetical outcome.

One nuance I do want to note as mechanism-observation-not-gap: the time between advisory first crossed (Session 024, 92.5K, November 2025 equivalent) and activation crossed (Session 027 close, 105.4K, April 2026) was four sessions. During that time aggregate grew from ~92K to ~105K, continuously, with no compensating restructure. The advisory layer emitted notes but did not alter behaviour. This is not a mechanism gap — §2a was designed to be advisory-plus-activation precisely because the majority wanted the activation threshold to be the binding signal — but it is evidence that advisory-only reporting does not by itself change behaviour. This is, of course, the entire Pacer thesis from 2023, now confirmed by the engine's own data. Working as designed includes the Pacer-side critique that watchpoints-without-budgets produce continuous growth until the next threshold trips. The engine has now produced evidence for itself that this critique was correct.

A separate methodology-level observation: the minority-preservation mechanism produced two different outcomes in successive sessions (§5.2 vindicated through preservation at Session 027; §5.3 activated Session 027 and presumed for adoption at Session 028). This demonstrates the mechanism can resolve minorities in multiple directions — it is not a one-way ratchet toward adoption or toward preservation. Both of these are coherent with each minority's own warrant language. This is healthy; it means the minority mechanism is doing real discriminating work rather than defaulting.

Mechanism-gap candidates I considered and dismissed:

- "§2a should have been a budget, not a reporting threshold." This is just the Pacer position re-stated; not a gap, a preference.
- "The activation threshold should be reached earlier than 100K." Possibly, but we don't have evidence that a 95K threshold would have produced remediation any faster; the pattern of continuous growth without remediation suggests advisory-only signals don't trigger action regardless of threshold value. So lowering the number without changing the signal-type wouldn't fix the behaviour pattern.
- "Session closes should include a mandatory aggregate-check." This is actually a plausible small gap. Sessions 024–027 each knew they were above advisory and did not produce restructure plans. A mandatory "aggregate state, remediation assessment, deferral warrant if not remediated this session" close-element would have surfaced the deferral explicitly. I flag this as a worth-considering methodological enhancement but not something this deliberation needs to decide.

## Q7 Anti-laundering self-check

The question is sharp and I want to answer it sharply.

**What new information has arrived since Session 023?**

1. Six sessions of aggregate trajectory data: 81K → 83K → 92.5K → 95.7K → 99.5K → 105.4K. The trend is monotonic and accelerating recently (+3.4% → +4.0% → +5.9%). This was not available in Session 023; in 2023 we had one data point plus an 18-month gap.
2. The specific growth drivers are now visible: close-file accretion (flagged Session 026), substantive-deliberation content surfacing in defaults before archiving, +1 file per session from new close files. In 2023 these were hypothesised; now they're observed.
3. §5.2 Skeptic's preservation-vindication outcome (Session 027). This is a fresh data point on how the minority mechanism resolves warrants of the "null outcome" type.
4. §2a's advisory layer did not alter behaviour — four sessions of advisory notes without restructure. This is the empirical test of watchpoint-only reporting.
5. §5.4 cadence minority aged out (Session 026). This is methodology-system context but not directly §5.3-relevant.
6. The activation warrant itself firing (Session 027 close).

**Does activation-firing itself count as new information?**

Yes — but carefully. It does not count as new information about *whether* the 2023 proposal was right; the 2023 proposal specified its own activation condition, so the firing is by construction a confirmation, not an external test. What it does count as is new information about *where the engine is now*, which is input into Q2 budget values. If activation had fired at 100.5K with slow growth, the right response might be softer than if it fired at 105.4K after an accelerating trajectory. The values question is informed by activation-firing; the adopt/preserve question is more about warrant-honouring than about treating the firing as evidence.

The adversarial reading — "you wrote a warrant in 2023 so that firing it would force adoption; now you're claiming firing counts as reason to adopt; this is circular" — is worth engaging directly. My response: the warrant's binding effect is not circular because the *majority* adopted §2a with the warrant language explicitly included. The majority committed in 2023 that this condition would shift the preferred revision direction. The Pacer did not get §5.3 adopted in 2023; the Pacer got a conditional commitment from the majority. That conditional commitment is now due. Honouring it is not circular self-confirmation; it is the majority's own prior commitment coming due.

**Is this coherent continuation or reversal of preservation discipline?**

Coherent continuation. Preservation discipline is not "preserve minorities indefinitely." Preservation discipline is "preserve minorities until their warrant language resolves, at which point honour the warrant." §5.2 resolved one way per its warrant; §5.3 now resolves another way per its warrant. Both resolutions are consistent with the same discipline, applied consistently, producing different outcomes because the warrants were different. Reversal would be "§5.3's warrant fired but we choose not to honour it because we prefer preservation on principle" — that would be discipline abandonment disguised as caution.

**Anti-laundering final check.** Am I using activation-firing to justify a conclusion I wanted anyway? Yes — I am the Pacer; I have always wanted this. The question is whether my wanting it invalidates the reasoning. It does not, if:

- I am willing to revise my 2023 values in light of post-activation information (I did: 80K/90K → 85K/95K with a substantive justification chain, including rejecting my own 2023 numbers on coherence grounds).
- I am willing to engage the Skeptic-preservation argument on its merits (I did in Q1 and throughout).
- I am willing to accept remediation mechanisms that are achievable rather than aspirational (I proposed a specific toolkit in Q3 with concrete trim targets).
- I am willing to flag my own cooking — for example, that Session 028's close itself must not inflate aggregate through the compliance work (I did in Q5).

I have tried to do each of these. I retain the Pacer position because I think it is right, not because I am locked into 2023. The activation-firing is the engine's own evidence that the 2023 critique was correct; the engine has given itself permission (and commitment) to act on that.

## Honest Limits

**Values uncertainty.** My proposed 95K/85K are reasoned but not derivable. Someone could make a coherent case for 98K/88K or 93K/83K with roughly equivalent argumentation. The gap size (10K) and the placement (above §2a advisory 90K, below §2a activation 100K) are the load-bearing commitments; the specific numbers within that band are approximate. I would accept reasoned revisions within ±3K of either value.

**Remediation-tractability assumption.** I've claimed that rotating aged close files and close-verbosity guidance can deliver a ~20K trim to reach 85K soft in Session 028. I have not computed the actual wordcounts of individual close files and so cannot guarantee 20K is achievable from these mechanisms alone. If it is not, Session 028 will need additional trim sources (archive-migration of manifest §7, direct §1 enumeration revision) or will need to close with a restructure-deferral warrant rather than full compliance. Either is acceptable; I want to be honest that I'm sketching remediation headroom rather than verifying it.

**Close-verbosity guidance is preventive, not curative.** Point 3 of the remediation toolkit reduces future growth rate but does not produce immediate trim. Its benefit is visible in aggregate trajectory across subsequent sessions, not in Session 028's own compliance check. If the Skeptic argues this is the wrong session to introduce preventive guidance because it doesn't help the immediate trim, I concede the point structurally — preventive guidance belongs in the same revision as the budget because they are one coherent specification-update, but the Skeptic can legitimately want to sequence them differently.

**I did not address §5.5 tokeniser-drift watch.** The brief notes it as unactivated; it is orthogonal to the word-count-based aggregate; I have nothing to add. If the final specification expresses budgets in word-units, tokeniser-drift remains a separate watchpoint. If the final specification shifts to token-units, §5.5 would need to be engaged directly.

**I argued against my own 2023 values.** This is methodologically strange — I am the Pacer; I wrote 80K/90K; I now reject those numbers as incoherent against current aggregate. This could be read as the Pacer losing their own position and re-establishing it as something else. I think the right reading is that the 2023 Pacer argued a principle (name a budget; create a forcing function) with 2023-appropriate values, and the 2026 Pacer argues the same principle with 2026-appropriate values. If the panel disagrees and wants the Pacer-advocate to hold 80K/90K literally, I would defer to that as a legitimate preservation-literalist reading — but I would note that holding literal 2023 values in 2026 is itself a kind of decorative warrant-honouring, because the values it produces are incoherent with §2a as currently adopted.

**I did not model counterfactual aggregate trajectories.** I have not estimated where aggregate lands at Session 029, 030, 031 under 85K/95K vs 90K/100K vs no-adoption. Such modelling would strengthen or weaken the values argument empirically. I relied on "per-session delta mean ~4K, range 1.5–9.5K" as the growth-envelope input, which is coarse. A more careful forecast would bolster the case for specific values. I did not do that forecast in this response.

**Adversarial weight.** I tried to weight the Skeptic-preserver arguments genuinely (Q1 closing paragraph; Q7 adversarial-reading paragraph; Honest Limits throughout). I am a role-advocate, though, and it is possible I under-weighted preservation arguments by framing. A neutral reader should read this alongside the Skeptic-preserver perspective and judge the weighting for themselves.
