## Q1 — Is identity threshold met?

**Three-way convergence (Namer, Steward, Outsider) on mature markers:**
- **The nine-activity kernel as a stable frame.** [01a, Q1]: "a recognisable silhouette — not a collection of practices, a specific frame." [01b, Q1]: "a frame that improves under its own discipline without losing its shape." [01d, Q1]: "the nine-activity kernel is no longer a loose aspiration; it has persisted long enough to function as a stable frame."
- **Preservation-by-versioning as distinctive.** [01a, Q1]: "This is rare. Most methodologies either freeze... or churn... This workspace treats superseded specs as durable provenance." [01b, Q1]: "This is not a logging habit; it is a constitutive commitment." [01d, Q1]: "unusually specific: versioned specs, explicit `supersedes` / `superseded-by` pointers... strong differentiator."
- **External-artefact track with n=1 validated.** [01a, Q1]: "not a proven general-purpose methodology." [01b, Q1]: "A name that foregrounded 'proven practice' or 'tested method' would over-claim." [01d, Q1]: "the method is not only describing itself; it is being exercised."

**Skeptic's counter at strongest** [01c, Q1]: *"Accumulation, not maturation. Decision count scales with session count mechanically — it measures throughput, not convergence."* And: *"A self-hosting system that has revised its own kernel in 2 of its last 3 sessions is not stable — it is successfully iterating."*

**Cross-model convergence on what is NOT yet mature:** three perspectives (Namer, Steward, Outsider) explicitly name external-adoption-absence and thin cross-domain track record as under-developed. [01a, Q1]: "External adoption... A name that implies 'framework for X' risks overclaiming." [01b, Q1]: "The scale of deliberation is narrow... has not been exercised by genuinely independent human perspectives at session-scale." [01d, Q1]: "Social identity outside the workspace is effectively absent."

**Synthesis Q1 position:** identity threshold is met for a **light-touch canonical name** — not for a brand layer, slogan, claim-of-school, or name that foregrounds dimensions still in formation (community, cross-domain breadth, domain-proven-practice). The Skeptic's refusal is preserved as a first-class minority position with four explicit falsifiability conditions [01c, Q5] — any of which would, if met, convert the refusal to support. The falsifiability conditions are substantially congruent with the other three perspectives' recorded thresholds for "clearly right versus merely defensible" naming.

**Observation:** The Namer (Claude) produced three internal-vocabulary compounds as its top-three (Record Kernel, Kernel Method, Session Kernel). This is the exact pattern Outsider predicted. The Steward (Claude), instructed to evaluate rather than generate, produced a single non-compound metaphor (Sediment). The Skeptic (Claude) produced no candidate. Outsider's top candidate (Selvedge) is a metaphor from a domain (textiles) with low collision risk in this space. Outsider's #3 (Strata) converges with Namer's #4 (Strata) — this is the only candidate proposed by both Claude and non-Claude perspectives independently, suggesting Strata is the cross-model common ground.

**Evaluation against Q3 criteria (cross-model consolidated weighting):**

Four perspectives independently weighted evaluation criteria [01a, 01b, 01c, 01d at Q3]. Cross-model convergence is strong on:
- **Identity-fidelity / honesty / resistance to overclaiming** — all four weight highest or near-highest.
- **Resistance-to-ossification / scope resilience** — all four weight highly.
- **Collision-avoidance** — all four weight significantly.
- **Memorability** — three of four (Namer, Steward, Outsider) weight low; Skeptic weights 0%.

**Filtering candidates against consolidated weighting:**

- **Record Kernel** — high internal fit BUT strong cross-training divergence concern (Outsider Q2 explicit flag); overfits to current kernel vocabulary; will age badly if kernel structure shifts. **Synthesis flag: strongest internal-vocabulary candidate but bears the Outsider's specific cross-training concern.**
- **Ninefold / Session Kernel / Kernel Method / The Nine / Provenance (bare)** — eliminated for reasons explicitly identified by two or more perspectives (ossification to count; ML collision; PROV collision; pre-commits to structural features in motion).
- **Selvedge** — Outsider top pick; low collision; covers self-hosting + preservation + multi-strand; external-metaphor rather than internal-vocabulary (passing Outsider's Q2 flag); moderate legibility concern ("some readers will not know the term immediately" [01d, Q3]).
- **Sediment** — Steward's candidate; strong preservation fit; modest Latin-root translatability; mechanic matches methodology (layers accumulate, remain legible, later sits on earlier without erasing).
- **Strata** — cross-model overlap (only candidate from both model families); strong preservation fit; collision concerns noted by both [01a, Q2]: "widely used — a social-tech company, multiple software products, a font" and [01d, Q2]: "high. I know many products and companies using Strata."
- **Braid / Trace / Loom** — Outsider's own rankings place these below Selvedge; higher collision risk or narrower framing.

**Shortlist for user ratification (three candidates):**

1. **Selvedge** — Outsider's top pick; metaphor from textiles; "self-edge" — the finished edge woven into cloth so it does not unravel. Emphasises self-hosting (the methodology weaves its own edges), multiple strands held together (perspectives preserved as distinct), and preservation against fraying (versioned specs not overwritten). Lowest collision risk of the three. Legibility cost: some readers will not know the word on first encounter. [01d, Q2 Q3].

2. **Sediment** — Steward's candidate; Latin root *sedimentum*, "a settling." Accumulated layers preserved over time; later layers sit on earlier without erasure; sequence readable backward. Mechanic matches the methodology's v1-v2-v3 preservation and decision-record accumulation. Accentuates preservation specifically; understates the active deliberation and external-production aspects. [01b, Q2 Q3 Q6].

3. **Strata** — only candidate produced by both Claude and non-Claude perspectives. Geological/archaeological: layers remain inspectable, accumulation preserves history. Similar accent to Sediment but more static-sounding; higher collision risk. Less distinctive than Selvedge. [01a, Q2] [01d, Q2].

Also preserved (but not recommended): **Record Kernel** (Namer top pick) — explicitly carries the Outsider-flagged cross-training concern; preserved as the Claude-family first preference for reference.

## Q4 — Placement

**Perspective positions:**

- Namer [01a, Q4]: **kernel frontmatter only** — *"the minimum placement that makes the name canonical... keeps Session 012 single-increment."* Rejects PROMPT.md (over-burdens workspace), new identity.md (overstates weight), no-canonical (doesn't resolve OI-001), SESSION-LOG (log is record, not spec).

- Steward [01b, Q4]: **kernel title + PROMPT.md + identity.md** — broadest placement. *"The name belongs where the methodology's load-bearing identity lives... If the name cannot survive being the kernel's title, the name is not ready."* Records tension: kernel-title ossifies most.

- Skeptic [01c, Q4]: **no placement (refuse)** — or, if overruled, *"option (c), the lightest available, with explicit provisional-status language"* — i.e., identity.md with "provisional" framing. Key observation: *"whichever placement the deliberation chooses reveals its true commitment."*

- Outsider [01d, Q4]: **`specifications/identity.md` only** — *"the smallest blast radius. It likely requires ordinary spec-addition coverage and provenance recording, but it should avoid D-023-triggering revisions to the kernel."* Rejects kernel-title (overstates "kernel" as defining organ), PROMPT.md-primary (too sticky and constitutive), no-canonical (functionally a refusal to name).

**Synthesis Q4 position — weighing the four placements:**

If naming proceeds, the Outsider's and Skeptic's (overruled-conditional) converge on **`specifications/identity.md` only** as the lightest defensible placement. The Namer's kernel-frontmatter-only objection to identity.md ("overstates the name's weight") is weighed against the Outsider's specific counter: *"it does not force a kernel v-bump merely to rename the umbrella under which the kernel sits... does not lock the methodology to design as a content domain."*

The Steward's three-place broad placement is the strongest-commitment option but bears tension the Steward itself records. The Namer's kernel-frontmatter is one placement up from the Outsider's.

**Cross-model convergence on avoiding PROMPT.md-first placement:** three perspectives (Namer, Skeptic, Outsider) reject PROMPT.md as primary placement. Only Steward endorses it, and as supplementary rather than primary.

**Three-way cross-model agreement on kernel-title/frontmatter avoidance OR minimal use:** Outsider and Skeptic both explicitly reject kernel-title canonicalisation (Outsider: *"overstates 'kernel' as the methodology's defining organ"*; Skeptic: *"most ossifying placement available"*). Namer allows kernel-frontmatter only (one-line `name:` field, not kernel title). Only Steward endorses kernel-title.

**Synthesis Q4 recommendation:** `specifications/identity.md` as primary placement. If the user prefers broader gravity, secondary placement is kernel frontmatter (`name:` field, not kernel title retitling). PROMPT.md first-paragraph revision is the strongest-commitment option and is recorded as available but not recommended.

## Q5 — The refuse-to-name case

**Skeptic's primary position preserved in full** [01c, Q5]:
> *"A name is a closure. It says: this thing is sufficiently this-thing to be called something. Every discipline the methodology has cultivated over eleven sessions argues against that closure now. Further — and this is the sharpest form — the methodology has been performing its own Skeptic discipline on its own naming question since Session 001. OI-001 has remained open for eleven sessions. That is not a bug or a deferral. That is the methodology doing exactly what it teaches: preserving a question at its strongest until it is genuinely ready to close. Closing it now because Session 012's primary work didn't arrive is inverting the discipline."*

**Skeptic's four explicit falsifiability conditions** [01c, Q5] — any one of these converts the refusal:
1. External adoption threshold: at least one named practitioner outside this workspace uses the methodology for at least three months.
2. Cross-domain artefact threshold: at least three externally validated artefacts across materially different domains.
3. Kernel-stability threshold: at least five consecutive sessions with no kernel revision.
4. External naming demand: an actual external reader or user requests a name.

**Cross-model convergence on falsifiability-condition content:** all four perspectives independently named substantially the same thresholds for "clearly right versus merely defensible." Namer [01a, Q5]: external adopter; third domain-validated artefact; name-absence failure mode. Steward [01b, Q5]: third cross-domain artefact; external adopter; vocabulary stability. Skeptic [01c, Q5]: the four conditions above. Outsider [01d, Q5]: external naming need; 3+ cross-domain artefacts; methodology survives one substantial self-revision; external adopter. **Strongest single cross-model convergence finding in Session 012's deliberation: the four perspectives independently identified external adoption and 3+ cross-domain artefacts as the evidence thresholds that would convert defensible-naming to clearly-right-naming. None of these thresholds is currently met.**

**Why the other three reject refusal:**

- Namer [01a, Q5]: *"I record here that the refuse-to-name case, seriously engaged, is approximately as strong as the name-now case, and a Session 012 decision to hold OI-001 open with the three conditions above would be defensible — possibly more defensible than naming."* Does not actually reject refusal; concludes it is comparably strong but still argues the recommended name. Honest ambivalence preserved.

- Steward [01b, Q5]: *"The Skeptic's claim about defensive attachment is real but hypothetical; the friction-tax is real and present."* Rejects on balance-of-costs-now grounds, with low confidence.

- Outsider [01d, Q5]: *"naming now satisfies the G/O/K/S package honestly through K and S... The work is load-bearing enough if handled minimally. The right move is not 'be proud of the name'; it is 'stop making the method refer to itself generically.'"*

**Synthesis Q5 position:** Refuse-to-name is preserved as a first-class minority position. The Skeptic's falsifiability conditions are incorporated into the methodology's open-issue record whether or not naming proceeds — they are the explicit triggers under which a post-naming Session-NN could reopen OI-001 as "name was premature," or under which a continued-refusal decision could be revisited as "conditions now met." The majority's decision to name is load-bearing under G/O/K/S K+S criteria [01d, Q5] but does not exceed "defensible" status.

**Brief-framing finding recorded:** Outsider [01d, Meta]: *"Q2 is concrete while Q5 is evidentiary, which subtly biases the synthesis toward naming."* Skeptic [01c, Meta]: *"Q5 is framed as a branch; it should be the trunk. The brief concedes 'deciding not to name' as a valid outcome but structures the questions as if naming is the default."* Both perspectives identify the same structural bias in the brief. This is a genuine finding; the synthesis applies its correction by refusing to rubber-stamp the "majority-favours-naming" conclusion. The majority view is "defensible naming with minimal placement"; the Skeptic view is "refuse with explicit falsifiability." Both are live options.

## Q6 — What naming changes

**Convergence across all four perspectives:** naming steers future self-presentation. Disagreement is only about whether the steering is acceptable for this methodology now.

- [01a, Q6]: Record Kernel accentuates the Record activity; *"subtly demotes the Decide activity."*
- [01b, Q6]: Sediment accentuates preservation; *"makes it harder for a future session to casually weaken the preservation discipline... name acting as a brake on drift."*
- [01c, Q6]: Any name restricts interpretive space; *"Treating a future kernel revision as disclosure-of-error rather than identity-update"* becomes harder named.
- [01d, Q6]: Selvedge steers toward *"version preservation, explicit provenance, and the combination of multiple strands without erasure."* Risk: *"tempt later sessions to overextend textile metaphors."*

**Synthesis Q6 position:** Whichever candidate is adopted steers future sessions toward its accent. **Selvedge** accents self-hosting + preservation + multi-strand, leaving nine-activity structure, validation-vs-production balance, and cross-model participation unprivileged. **Sediment** accents preservation specifically, with a "brake on drift" protection. **Strata** accents preservation similarly but more statically. **Refuse** steers toward continued modesty at the cost of the friction-tax and the informal de-facto-naming risk [01d, Q6].
