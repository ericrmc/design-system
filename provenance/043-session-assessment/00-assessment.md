> - A periodic self-audit at some cadence where the assessment reads further back than the default retention window and reconsiders the engine's path-selection bias from a longer baseline.
> - Recognition of work-shapes the current defaults don't generate: evolutionary increments without an activation warrant; exercising under-tested machinery (external-problem mode in another workspace; non-GPT non-Claude lineages per §5.6); multi-session investigations where constraints aren't fully known up-front and emerge across the run.

> - The reasoned conclusion that the current discipline is correct and my framing is wrong — preserve that as a first-class minority if the deliberation lands there.

> The deliberation should likely involve a perspective whose explicit role is to propose work-shapes the current default doesn't generate, and at least one non-Claude lineage (which would also be the §5.6 second-of-6 observational data point cast on the vindication side rather than the worst-case side). Read further back than the 6-session retention window — the absence of warrant-firing across the engine's history is itself the data point.
>
> Your output may be a specification change, a new convention, a new OI, or rejection of the framing. The deliberation is the ask.
