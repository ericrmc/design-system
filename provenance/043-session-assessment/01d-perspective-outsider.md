## Q1 through Q7

### Q1. Is warrant-firing genuinely sparse by design, or under-articulated?

**Neither, exactly.** The framing hides the real shape: warrant-firing is **sparse against one class of input and dense against another**. It is dense against engine-internal trajectories (§5.2 vindicated at 5-session runway; §5.3 activation at 100K aggregate; §5.6–§5.11 all with specific numerical triggers; §10.4-M1 through M6 with explicit windows). It is **sparse against the operator's own frame-level observations about the engine** — there is no warrant whose firing condition is "the operator has noticed a pattern the engine has not represented to itself." The operator's question is exactly such an observation, and it fired nothing; it had to be routed in via the operator-surfaced-agenda class (Session 036 Path PD precedent). That is not "sparse by design" in the clean sense the Defender will want to use; it is a **gap in the input surface** the engine accepts warrant-signal from.

Call this: **the engine's warrant surface is closed against its operator's frame observations.** The operator can provide data (reference texts, external constraints) and directives (single-token path ratifications), but the operator's standing role as *frame critic of the engine* has no spec-level ingestion mechanism. It depends on operator persistence and on the Case Steward being willing to classify an expanded input as Path-PD-class. That is a real asymmetry.

### Q2. Metrics: evidence of discipline working / deferring / neither / both?

**Both, and the distinction is recoverable by category.** WX-24-1 MAD 20-session no-growth is evidence of **spec stability** (a null result; no one had reason to change it), not of a discipline exercised. Engine-v7 preservation-window depth 6 is evidence of **v-bump restraint**, which §5.4 asked for — that one is discipline working, but §5.4 is about bump cadence, not about work shape. Zero-event §10.4-M1/M2/M5 windows are evidence of **event-absence**, a fact about the world.

The metrics read as positive because they are metrics of *restraint-disciplines specifically*. The engine has no positively-framed metric that reports *non-restraint* successes — "work surfaced that would not have surfaced without an engine mechanism" has no place to land in the current reporting surface. That asymmetry is the self-referential trap §5 of the brief warns against, operationalised.

### Q3. Work-shapes default-agent reasoning does not generate

The Proposer will enumerate these. I will name the category that *makes the enumeration possible*: **the default-agent's vocabulary of work-shapes is inherited from precedent shapes**. Path A / Path L / Path J / Path PD / Path OI-004 all exist because a prior session exhibited them and a Case Steward labelled them. No session has ever opened with a shape the engine had not previously instantiated except via operator-surfaced agenda (S036 Path PD). **The engine has no generator for novel work-shapes; it has only a recogniser for shapes it has already seen.** The recogniser is sophisticated and the precedent set is now rich (~10 labelled paths), but the absence of a generator is the thing.

This is a defect only if generating novel shapes is load-bearing. It is load-bearing if there exist engine-improving shapes the precedent set does not cover. By construction, the engine cannot know the answer to that from inside — which is why the operator's observation matters.

### Q4. Rejected-alternatives for path recommendation?

The Defender will argue this is a laundering mechanism: forcing alternatives creates pressure to activate them. That argument has weight; it is the structural dual of the "laundering the activation" critique I contributed at Session 028.

But note what's different: at Session 028, the laundering-risk was *moving a numerical threshold* after it had been crossed, to re-admit the pre-crossing state. Here, the analogous risk would be *lowering the bar for non-Path-A paths* so they look easier to activate. **Rejected-alternatives with evidence does not lower the bar** — it raises the bar on Path-A selection by requiring it to be argued-for against enumerated alternatives, which is a higher standard than Path-A-by-default.

The real risk is different: **rejected-alternatives becomes ritual.** Decision records already have Rejected alternatives; in ~40 sessions, one can inspect whether those sections ever changed a decision. My honest read from the record is that they are mostly demonstrations that alternatives were considered, not hinges on which decisions turned. A rejected-alternatives requirement at path-selection could degrade into the same ritual.

**My position:** introducing rejected-alternatives at path-selection is acceptable only if paired with a counter-mechanism: some sessions should *actually pick* a non-Path-A alternative on the basis of the enumeration, not just record it. The condition for that is itself a design question I do not think the engine has enough signal to answer yet.

### Q5. Periodic self-audit — net-positive or laundering?

The question mis-frames. A *periodic* audit at fixed cadence is laundering-shaped: it creates a scheduled "look harder" event that generates activity by calendar rather than by need. §5.3 was strongly vindicated at S041 precisely because calendar-triggered-checks had been papering over a question the engine could not close.

The operator's framing reveals a different need: **not periodic, but cross-sectional**. The engine's existing self-observation is session-local (each session reads the last 6 closes). It has no mechanism that reads across the distribution of session-shapes over full history and asks whether that distribution is the one the engine wants. Session 027's §C Archivist minority named this as "single-session self-audits cannot catch cross-sectional drift" — still preserved, still un-operationalised.

**Proposal:** the right unit is **cross-sectional audit with a triggering condition** — e.g., "when Path-A-shape exceeds 60% of the last 10 sessions without an operator-surfaced-agenda event." Warrant-gated (not laundering-shaped), cross-sectional (catches what session-local reading can't), falsifiable. The operator's six-of-ten is the data point that would have fired such a warrant. Not coincidence.

### Q6. Does §5.3 S041 vindication imply forward revision?

**Yes, and the discharge-as-vindicated language partially obscures this.** §5.3 was framed as "indefinitely movable finish line" — a warning that OI-004 closure would keep getting deferred. At S041 closure, the warning was judged accurate *about the past* (20 sessions of deferral happened) and discharged *because the event it warned against has now occurred and been remediated*. That framing is coherent for OI-004 specifically.

But §5.3's underlying diagnosis — **"a criterion whose finish line the engine keeps moving forward is not actually a criterion"** — generalises beyond OI-004. The engine's current warrants that have this shape include:

- Close-rotation retention-exception watchpoints (WX-28-1: "3 retention-exceptions in 10 sessions" — evaluated across a window that extends if no exceptions fire).
- §10.4-M1/M2/M5 evaluation windows (extending from Session 037 as data-points-continue-zero).
- OI-002 substantive-vs-minor accumulation (data-points accumulate indefinitely with no closure condition).

**Discharge-as-vindicated is complete for §5.3-as-OI-004-warrant. It is not complete as a methodology observation.** A session-generalising variant of §5.3 should be preserved — not as a minority on a specific decision, but as a standing methodology watchpoint at the level of `methodology-kernel.md` §Self-Hosting: "warrants whose firing conditions extend with non-firing data-points are indistinguishable from absent warrants." I do not propose that this session adopt that language; I propose that the operator's question is partially about it.

### Q7. Overall verdict on operator framing

**Partially correct; the wrong thing is "path-selection."** "Path-selection bias" is the wrong name: the phenomenon is not about Path A vs non-Path-A, it is about what *counts as a warrant*. "Self-reinforcing loop" is right but under-specified: the loop is not in the engine's behavior, it is in the *epistemics* — the engine cannot distinguish "nothing warrants change" from "change-warranting inputs are outside the warrant surface."

**Kept:** the factual observation (6 of 10 Path A); the diagnostic concern (observational-metric vindication cannot falsify itself); the §5.3-vindication-should-have-fed-forward point.

**Adjusted:**
- "Path-selection bias" → **warrant-surface closure against operator-frame observations**. Changes what the question is about: not "should we pick more non-A paths" but "what counts as a warrant, and who gets to raise one."
- "Self-reinforcing loop" → **epistemic self-ratification**. The engine's positive metrics cannot by construction report on what the engine has not noticed. That is not a loop; it is a blind spot by design.
- "Output categories" — the operator's four candidates all sit inside the existing frame. A fifth is missing: **spec-level enlargement of the warrant surface** — an "operator-frame-observation" input class alongside operator-direction and operator-surfaced-agenda, with its own path-class mapping. Distinct from operator-surfaced-agenda (which requires the operator to propose the *shape* of the work); a frame-observation input would require the operator only to state the observation, with the engine obligated to generate a deliberation shape.

## Independent claim

The question is not about path-selection or self-reinforcement; it is about **the closure of the engine's warrant surface against operator-frame observations**. The engine's warrant machinery treats operator inputs as directives, agenda-surfacers, or reference-text providers; it has no category for "the operator has noticed a frame-level property of the engine that the engine has not represented to itself." When such observations arrive, they route in via Path-PD-class by Case-Steward discretion, not by spec. The forward revision that honours the §5.3 vindication is not rejected-alternatives at path-selection; it is **a new operator-input class (operator-frame-observation) with its own path-class, or equivalently a cross-sectional warrant triggered by distribution properties the engine cannot see session-locally.**
