Let your eyes close, or leave them softly open, whichever feels less effortful.

Notice the sound of your own breath. Don't change it. Just listen to it as if you were listening to someone else in the room.

Now feel the points where your body meets the floor. Start at the back of your head. Is it touching? How much of it? A coin-sized patch, or a saucer, or something larger?

Move your attention down to your shoulders. Both of them. Is one more pressed into the floor than the other? Don't correct anything. Just register what is.

Now the ribs at the back. Some of them are probably touching the floor. Some are lifted away. Which ones? On which side?

Your lower back. Is there a gap between your lumbar spine and the floor? How big? Could you slide a hand under? A whole arm?

Your pelvis. The two sitting bones, the sacrum at the back. Is the weight even across them, or tipped to one side?

The backs of your thighs. Do they rest heavily, or do they hover?

The calves. The heels. Are both heels touching? Equally?

Now come back up to your head. Has anything changed in the last thirty seconds, just from your attention passing through?

Let your attention rest on the whole back surface of your body at once. The whole map of contact. Don't try to hold it all clearly. Just allow the sense of it.

Now, very slowly, let your head roll a little to the right. Not far. Just begin the movement. Feel the weight of your skull shift on the floor.

And back to centre. Slowly.

And to the left. Same small amount. Notice: is this direction different? Easier, harder, smoother, bumpier? Does your jaw want to come with it? Your eyes? Your tongue?

Back to centre.

Do this a few times, at your own pace, with long pauses between. Right. Centre. Left. Centre.

Notice what you're doing with your breath while you do this. Are you holding it? At which point?

Notice what your hands are doing. Your feet. Your face.

Now pause. Let the head rest in the middle.

Ask yourself, without answering in words: which side was easier?

Now, keeping the head still, let one shoulder lift very slightly off the floor. Just a millimetre. Whichever shoulder you chose. Don't decide in advance — notice which one offered itself.

Put it back down.

The other shoulder. Lift. A millimetre. Put it back down.

Was that the same movement, mirrored? Or did they feel like two different actions?

Again. The first shoulder. Then the second.

Pause.

Now let your whole attention drop back to the floor contact. The whole back of you. Has the shape of your contact changed since you started?

Where is there more floor than there was? Where is there less?

Don't judge it. Don't fix it. Just notice.

Take one slow breath in, and one slow breath out, and stay here.
