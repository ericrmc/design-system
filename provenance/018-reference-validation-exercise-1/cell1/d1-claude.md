# Facilitator's Brief: 12-Minute Silent-to-Synthesis Pattern

**Total:** 12 minutes | **Group:** 12 adults | **Structure:** 1 -> 2 -> 4 -> 12

---

## Segment 1: Silent Individual Reflection (2:00)

**Say (0:00):**
"I'm going to pose the question, then we sit in silence. No writing prompts, no discussion. Write in your own notebook whatever comes — fragments, objections, questions, drawings. Two minutes. When I ring the chime, finish your sentence and stop. The question is: [pose question clearly, then repeat it once]. Begin now."

**Watch for:** Anyone looking up for signals — give a small nod and return to your own stillness. Your visible calm sets the container.

**Say at 1:45:** "Fifteen seconds."

**Chime at 2:00.**

---

## Segment 2: Pairs (3:00)

**Say (2:00):**
"Turn to the person beside you. One pair of three if we have an odd seat. You have three minutes together. Here's the constraint: spend the first ninety seconds where Partner A speaks and Partner B only listens — no interrupting, no agreeing, no questions. Then swap. I'll call the switch. Goal isn't to agree — it's to hear what the other noticed that you didn't. Decide who goes first. Go."

**Say at 3:30:** "Switch. Partner B now speaks, Partner A listens."

**Say at 4:50:** "Ten seconds to land your final thought."

**Chime at 5:00.**

---

## Segment 3: Quads (3:30)

**Say (5:00):**
"Combine with the pair nearest you — groups of four. Three and a half minutes. Your job now is different: don't just share what you each said. Find the *tension* between your four perspectives. Where do you disagree, or where does one person's idea complicate another's? Appoint a scribe to capture one sentence that names the sharpest tension your group surfaced. One sentence. Not a summary — a tension. Go."

**Watch for:** Groups defaulting to round-robin sharing. Intervene gently at 6:30 if needed: "Remember — you're hunting for the tension, not taking turns."

**Say at 8:00:** "Thirty seconds. Lock in your one sentence."

**Chime at 8:30.**

---

## Segment 4: Whole-Group Synthesis (3:30)

**Say (8:30):**
"We come back together. Each scribe, in turn, reads only your one sentence — no elaboration, no defense. I'll write them on the board as you speak. Go in the order I point. Scribe one, please."

**Capture all three tensions verbatim on a visible surface. Do not editorialize. Takes ~90 seconds.**

**Say (10:00):**
"Look at what's on the board. I'm not asking you to resolve these. I'm asking: what is the *shape* of what we just surfaced? Is there a deeper question underneath all three tensions? A pattern? A shared assumption that's now visible? Anyone — call it out. No hands, just speak when moved."

**Hold silence if it comes. Silence after a real question is productive. Resist filling it for up to fifteen seconds.**

**As contributions come, your job is only to connect:** "That echoes what [name] said about..." or "So we have X on one side and Y on the other — is that the axis?"

**Say at 11:40:**
"Last twenty seconds. If you had to name what we now see that we didn't see twelve minutes ago, in a phrase — what is it?"

**Take two or three final phrases. Do not summarize yourself.**

**Chime at 12:00.**

**Say:** "Thank you. We'll leave it there."

---

## Facilitator notes

- Keep your own voice low and slow — the room mirrors your tempo.
- The chime (not your voice) owns transitions. Voice is for instruction only.
- If a segment runs long, cut the next one, not this one. Never steal from synthesis.
- The pattern's engine is the constraint in each segment: *silent* alone, *listening* in pairs, *tension-finding* in quads, *pattern-naming* in the whole. Name the constraint every time.
