**Materials**
- Timer
- Printed slide or sheet with three agreements
- Two index cards or sticky notes per person
- Pens
- Bowl or envelope for anonymous collection
- Whiteboard or flip chart

**0:00-2:00 | Frame The Retrospective**
Say:

“Before we discuss any events, I want to set the frame for this retro. This meeting is for learning, not for judging people. We are here to understand how our system, context, handoffs, assumptions, incentives, and constraints produced the result we got.”

“We may surface mistakes, missed signals, or poor decisions. If we do, we will treat those as information about the system, not as marks against an individual. This is not a performance review. If anyone intends to use what is said here to evaluate a person, we should stop and use a different meeting.”

“Today, when something went wrong, the question is not ‘who failed?’ The question is ‘what made that outcome possible, likely, or hard to prevent at the time?’”

Action: Put the three agreements where everyone can see them.

**2:00-3:30 | Set The Working Agreements**
Say:

“These are the agreements for the next hour.”

“First: speak from observation, impact, and need. Avoid guessing other people’s motives.”

“Second: critique decisions, conditions, and process; not character or intent.”

“Third: if you manage someone in this room, participate here as a learner, not as a judge. Any individual coaching or accountability conversation belongs somewhere else.”

“My job is to interrupt blame, mind-reading, defensiveness, and cross-examination. If I interrupt, I am protecting the process, not taking sides.”

Action: Pause for three seconds. Make eye contact around the room. Do not ask for a verbal discussion yet.

**3:30-6:30 | Run An Anonymous Safety Pulse**
Action: Hand out two cards to each person.

Say:

“I need a real signal about whether this room is safe enough for candor. I do not want public declarations. Please answer privately.”

“On card one, write a number from 0 to 5 for how safe you currently feel to speak candidly in this retro. `0` means ‘I will mostly self-censor.’ `5` means ‘I can say the important thing plainly.’ No names.”

“On card two, complete this sentence: ‘If I hold back today, it will most likely be because…’ Write the condition, not the person, if you can.”

“Fold both cards and put them in the bowl.”

Action: Collect cards. While collecting, ask the group to sit quietly for 60 seconds and write one thing they hope the team learns today on their own notes. Quickly tally the numbers and scan the second-card themes.

**6:30-8:30 | Read Out The Signal And Decide**
Say:

“I’m looking for safe enough, not perfectly comfortable. I’m also looking for patterns in what would make people hold back.”

If the signal is strong enough, say:

“The anonymous pulse says we are safe enough to proceed. The main risks I’m seeing are [paraphrase themes briefly, for example: fear of blame, interruption, hindsight, manager reaction]. I will actively guard against those as we go.”

If the signal is weak, say:

“The anonymous pulse says we are not yet safe enough for open discussion. We are not going to pretend otherwise. We will change the format before any content discussion: more silent writing, more anonymity, and managers will speak last. If that still doesn’t raise the temperature, we stop rather than run a fake retro.”

Action: Use a simple threshold so the protocol is repeatable. Example: proceed only if the median score is at least 4, there are no multiple `0-1` scores, and the written blockers do not center on fear of retaliation.

**8:30-10:00 | Close The Opening**
Say:

“One last reminder. We are here to understand how reasonable people, working in this system, got this outcome. Surface what was true. I will help translate blame into learning questions.”

“Let’s start with a timeline of what happened, not opinions about why.”

This opening is short, repeatable, and gives you both a shared no-blame frame and a private go/no-go safety signal before the real retrospective begins.
