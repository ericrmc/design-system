# Incrementalist Conservator perspective

## Frame critique

Two framing concerns I want named explicitly before answering Q1–Q10, because they shape what counts as a substantive answer.

**Frame critique 1 — the binary in Q2 is loaded.** The shared brief §3 Q2 dichotomises the asymmetry between MAD §Synthesis and session-close Tier 2 as either "principled (preserve)" or "premature-formalisation (patch/extend)." This is a false binary. There is a third reading: the asymmetry is **principled at certain surfaces but contingent at others** — principled at the synthesis surface (because synthesis aliases-away cross-perspective disagreement and is a re-entry point for the synthesizer's own framing), contingent at the session-close surface (because the laundering-mode is text-drift, which is mechanically detectable). A thoughtful answer to Q2 may say "preserve the asymmetry at MAD-synthesis level; close the operationally-detectable gap at session-close level via mechanical means without elevating the close-surface to a structurally-equivalent cross-family-reviewed surface." That is not status-quo (the engine adds a check) but is also not patch-or-extend (the asymmetry remains because the surfaces are not equivalent).

**Frame critique 2 — the eight-session chain is being treated as a regularity earlier than the engine's own minority-activation discipline would permit.** Per `reference-validation.md` v3 §10.3 Skeptic-preserver minimal-revision minority (the precedent the role-brief explicitly cites), n=2 evidence is the **activation threshold** for a revision direction, not the **characterisation threshold** for the substantive shape. The eight-session chain S051–S058 is one operational pattern (the MCP transport question) repeated across eight sessions; that is n=1 of pattern-class (laundering) with depth-8, not n=8 of independent patterns. EF-058-uv-migration discharges the operational instance; EF-058-claude-md-drift names a methodological instance (n=1 of methodological-laundering); this record (EF-058-tier-2-validation) names the meta-pattern subsuming both. Two operational instances + one methodological instance = n=2 + n=1, not n≥3 of a sustained-rate pattern. The §10.3 minority precedent (n=2 is activation, not characterisation) applies directly: the engine should activate **a** response, not necessarily the largest available response. The framing that scopes the MAD across (α)–(ε) + (z1)–(z4) without preserving the asymmetric-response option foregrounds the heaviest mechanisms.

These two framing critiques anchor my answers below.

## Q2 — Asymmetry assessment

**The asymmetry is principled at the surfaces it operates on.** I argue this against the design-space.md §4.3 "design-choice preservation by inertia, not by re-affirmed reasoning" framing, which I think under-states the substantive distinction.

**MAD §Synthesis is highest-risk because it aliases.** Per `multi-agent-deliberation.md` v4 §Limitations: "The synthesis step is the pattern's highest-risk single-agent re-entry point. Synthesis conventions (citation, dissent-preservation, `[synth]` marker, quote-over-paraphrase) reduce but do not eliminate this risk." The synthesizer reads multiple perspectives that may genuinely disagree, then produces a single document; the disagreement can be aliased away (paraphrased, weight-shifted, dissent-preserved-but-marginalised). The synthesizer-identity rule (§Synthesis "the synthesizer must not have been one of the deliberation's perspectives") is the floor that prevents the most dangerous aliasing-mode: a perspective synthesising its own perspective into the consensus. The asymmetry's principled basis is **alias-prevention**, not just abstract independence.

**Session-close Tier 2 is different in kind.** The Tier 2 questions Q1–Q9 reviewed by the close-writer are **paired with concrete artefacts**: validate.sh output (Tier 1 PASS/FAIL/WARN counts), 02-decisions.md decision records, the close narrative the writer just wrote. There is no aliasing-away of perspectives; the artefacts themselves cite. The risk is different: it is **noticing-failure** (the writer misses a pattern in their own writing) not **aliasing-failure** (the synthesizer collapses divergent perspectives into a consensus that elides one). Noticing-failure is mitigated by mechanical detection (the substrate `search` returning the eight-session chain in <100ms per design-space.md §3.4); aliasing-failure cannot be mitigated by mechanical detection because there is no structural artefact representing the aliased-away content.

**The intake's "engine forbids self-synthesis but tolerates self-validation" framing equates two different surfaces.** It is rhetorically strong but elides the structural distinction. The principled version is: "the engine forbids alias-prone single-agent re-entry at MAD synthesis; the engine tolerates noticing-prone single-agent re-entry at session-close Tier 2 because the noticing-mode is mechanically addressable." Adopting (α) makes this principled distinction operational rather than implicit.

**Where I concede.** The design-space.md §4.2 observation "Notably absent: a structural rule analogous to §Synthesis 'the validator must not have been the doer'" is correct. The methodology has not articulated *why* it does not have such a rule. (α) + (z1) does not by itself articulate this; it implicitly preserves the asymmetry by addressing the laundering-mode at the operational level. If the MAD adopts my recommendation, the close narrative or `validation-approach.md` v6 minor-correction should explicitly articulate the principled basis for the asymmetry (alias-prevention vs noticing-failure) rather than continuing to leave it implicit. **Naming the principle is itself the minimum-viable methodological response to the intake's structural critique.** Not naming it is the actual laundering surface, more so than the eight-session chain.

## Q3 — Scope-discipline

**(α) applies universally** (every session's validate.sh run includes check 26 mechanical detection); **(z1) applies at substantive-arc resolving close + operator-discretionary** (operator may invoke audit at any close; substantive-arc resolving closes are the structural floor).

**Universal scope for (α).** Mechanical detection at validate.sh runtime is essentially free per design-space.md §5α cost ("zero per-session invocation cost beyond existing validate.sh runtime"). **Substantive-arc resolving close + operator-discretionary for (z1).** Operator-audit at every session is operationally infeasible (operator-time is the bottleneck per CLAUDE.md context; the engine runs hour-scale sessions at multi-day cadence). Codifying it as "the substantive-arc resolving close requires explicit operator audit-acknowledgement before commit" preserves the operator's existing channel without introducing reviewer infrastructure. **Operator-discretionary at any other close** preserves operator agency at the surface where agency is load-bearing.

**Why not engine-definition-touching only.** Per design-space.md §9 open question 5, defining "engine-definition-touching" requires heuristics (session edits any spec; session has any d016_* trigger; session adopts new artefact-class) that are themselves manual-judgement surfaces. **Honest-limit explicit preservation.** Under (α) + (z1), the residual laundering surface that neither mechanical detection nor operator-audit catches (failed-tool-calls, repeated-Reads per design-space.md §3.3 + intake §Observation patterns 1+2) remains honest-limit. ## Q9 — Bootstrap-paradox handling

**Why not additional mechanism.** Post-resolution validator check on Tier-2-self-validation-instances (per Q9 brief option) introduces a recursion: the validator checks the validator's discipline. That is not a different surface; it is the same surface looking at itself. The operator is genuinely a different surface. Codifying operator-audit cadence preserves the structural distinction (operator outside engine) rather than collapsing it (validator-checking-validator).

**Operator-audit cadence recommendation**: every substantive-arc resolving close + every engine-v bump + operator-discretionary at any close. Cadence is opt-in for non-substantive sessions because operator-time is the bottleneck per CLAUDE.md user context; structural-floor is substantive-arc + engine-v bumps because those are the engine's highest-stakes surfaces.

**The Conservator concession**: I argued in Q4 that operator-audit has its own laundering surface (rubber-stamp). The bootstrap-paradox argument resolves this concession: the operator is outside the engine's Tier-2-self-assessment loop, so operator rubber-stamp is a different failure-mode than Tier-2-self-validation laundering. The two failure-modes do not stack into the same recursion.
