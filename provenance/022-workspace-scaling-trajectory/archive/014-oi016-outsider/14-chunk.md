Five short moves, arranged as a loose cycle, with a narrow side-strip for “if one of you cares more” and “what this refuses.” If it spills into a second page, that second page should be examples or notes, not more machinery.\n\nThe reason to prefer a loop over a linear checklist is specific to this domain. Name the actual decision.**  \nThe first move is: “What are we deciding, exactly?” and “Why are we deciding it now?” That sounds obvious, but it prevents the common drift from one concrete choice into a sprawling referendum on taste, spending, or the whole house. “Shelving for the lounge” is a decision. “What kind of people are we as homeowners?” is not.\n\nVerbatim wording I would consider: **“Keep the decision small enough to finish.”**\n\n**2.
