# Admission Procedure for Permanent Membership

## Stage 1: Approach and Sponsorship

The applicant must be introduced by an existing member in good standing of at least three years' tenure. The sponsor submits a written declaration to the council of elders stating their willingness to vouch for the applicant and accept reputational consequences should the admission prove harmful. No applicant may approach the community directly without sponsorship.

## Stage 2: Declaration of Intent

The applicant submits a written statement covering: their reasons for seeking permanent membership, their understanding of the community's purpose and obligations, prior affiliations they will retain or sever, and acknowledgement that admission is irreversible. The statement is read aloud at the next general assembly and entered into the record.

## Stage 3: Probationary Residence

The applicant lives among the community for a minimum of twelve months under provisional status. During this period they hold no voice in deliberations, may not speak in formal assemblies, and are assigned to work alongside members from each of the community's principal divisions. A designated mentor, distinct from the sponsor, records observations weekly.

## Stage 4: Examination by the Council of Elders

At the close of probation, the council convenes a private examination. Questions cover the community's history, its founding obligations, the applicant's conduct during probation, and any matters raised by the mentor's record. The applicant must answer without preparation. The council deliberates in camera and produces a written finding.

## Stage 5: Open Objection Period

The council's finding is posted publicly for thirty days. Any member may lodge a formal objection, which must be specific, signed, and submitted in writing. Anonymous objections are disregarded. Each objection is heard by a panel of three elders not previously involved, who may interview the objector, the applicant, and any witnesses.

## Stage 6: General Assembly Vote

The full membership convenes. The sponsor presents the case for admission. The applicant addresses the assembly briefly. Any unresolved objections are read. The vote requires a supermajority — no fewer than four-fifths of voting members present, with quorum of two-thirds of the total membership. A single negative vote from a council elder triggers automatic deferral of one year, after which the procedure must restart from Stage 4.

## Stage 7: Oath and Investiture

Upon affirmative vote, the applicant takes the binding oath before the assembly, naming the obligations they assume and the consequences of breach. The sponsor stands beside them and re-affirms their guarantee. The oath is recorded in the community's permanent register, signed by the applicant, the sponsor, and the senior elder present.

## Stage 8: Integration

The new member is assigned a position consistent with their probationary record. Full voice in deliberations vests immediately. The sponsor's guarantee remains active for a further three years.
