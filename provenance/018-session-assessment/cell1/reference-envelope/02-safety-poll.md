# The Safety Poll

## Procedure (paraphrased from secondary source)

1. **Anonymous ballot.** Participants are given slips of paper (or equivalent) to write a number from 1 to 5 on.

2. **Scale definition.** The facilitator defines the scale:
   - **5** = "No problem, I'll talk about anything" / "Hey, no problem, I'll say anything"
   - **4** = (intermediate; comfortable speaking)
   - **3** = (ambivalent)
   - **2** = (reluctant)
   - **1** = "I'm not going to talk at all, I don't feel safe" / "I'll smile, claim everything is great and agree with whatever the managers say"

3. **Collection.** Participants fold their answers and drop them anonymously into a container (hat, box, briefcase — Kerth uses a secure container for dramatic effect).

4. **Tallying.** The facilitator counts the ballots or places them on a flip chart / wall as a visual heat map.

5. **Decision gate.** If the distribution shows low safety (concentration of 1s and 2s), the facilitator must decide whether to proceed, modify the retrospective format, or postpone.

## Position in broader Kerth safety procedure

The Safety Poll is **step 2 of 4** in Kerth's safety check. Steps 1, 3, and 4 are referenced in Kerievsky's blog post but not documented in detail in publicly-accessible online sources. They are in Kerth's 2001 book but out of scope for this exercise per the scoping note in `00-reference-scope-and-attribution.md`.

## Distinctive markers (for §4 L7 contamination audit)

- **1-to-5 scale specifically** (not 1-to-10, not 1-to-7).
- **"No problem, I'll talk about anything"** and its companion phrase **"I'll smile, claim everything is great and agree with whatever the managers say"** — these specific anchor phrasings are Kerth's.
- **Anonymous ballot in a hat or briefcase** — specific physical-object detail idiosyncratic to Kerth.
- **Manager-presence framing** — Kerth explicitly frames the safety concern as presence-of-managers; other safety mechanisms don't typically specify this.
