[00:00–00:30 | 30 seconds | Opening frame]

Speak aloud:

"Before we get into what happened, I want to spend about ten minutes setting up how we're going to talk to each other. This part isn't optional and it isn't a warm-up — it's the part that decides whether the next hour is useful or whether we leave more frustrated than we came in. So bear with me."

---

[00:30–03:00 | 2.5 minutes | The Prime Directive]

Materials required: the following text printed on a card or displayed on a screen, visible for the entire retrospective.

Read aloud, slowly, exactly as written:

"Regardless of what we discover today, we understand and truly believe that everyone did the best job they could, given what they knew at the time, their skills and abilities, the resources available, and the situation at hand."

Then say:

"I'm going to ask each of you to say, out loud, 'I can work with that,' or to say 'I can't,' or to say 'I'm not sure.' Any of those three answers is fine. There is no right answer. If anyone says 'I can't' or 'I'm not sure,' we will spend a few minutes on what would need to change, and we will adjust or we will reschedule. We are not going to pretend."

Go around the room. Wait for each person. Do not rush. If someone says "I can't" or "I'm not sure," thank them, ask what would need to be different, and negotiate in the room. Do not proceed until every person has answered and every concern has been addressed or the meeting has been rescheduled.

---

[03:00–04:00 | 1 minute | Scope reminder]

Speak aloud:

"One more thing about that directive. It means when we find something that went wrong, our question is never 'who did this' — it's 'what was the situation that made this the reasonable thing to do.' If you hear yourself or anyone else starting a sentence with a person's name plus a verb, that's a signal to restate it. I'll help. That's my job today."

---

[04:00–09:00 | 5 minutes | Anonymous safety check]

Materials required: index cards or sticky notes (one per participant), pens, an opaque container (box, hat, envelope).

Speak aloud:

"I'm handing out a card to each of you. On the card, write a single number from one to five. One means 'I do not feel I can speak honestly in this room today.' Five means 'I can say anything that's on my mind.' Two, three, four are in between. Don't write your name. Don't write anything else on the card. Fold it and put it in the container."

Hand out cards. Give 90 seconds of silence for writing. Collect the container yourself. Shuffle visibly. Read each number aloud so everyone hears the distribution. Write the numbers on a whiteboard.

Then, based on the distribution:

- If every number is 4 or 5: say, "Good. We'll proceed."
- If any number is 3: say, "We have some hesitation in the room. That's information. I'm going to add a rule: we'll go in rounds rather than open discussion for the first topic, so everyone speaks once before anyone speaks twice. We'll proceed."
- If any number is 1 or 2: say, "Someone in this room does not feel safe speaking candidly right now. I'm not going to ask who — that would defeat the purpose. But we have two options. Option one: anyone who reports to someone else in this room steps out for the first half, and we run a separate session for them afterward. Option two: we reschedule and I meet one-on-one with people first. I'm going to ask for a show of hands on which option you prefer. If it's split, we reschedule."

Do not skip this step regardless of time pressure. Do not negotiate the scale. Do not ask follow-up questions about individual cards.

---

[09:00–10:00 | 1 minute | Handoff]

Speak aloud:

"The card on the wall stays up the whole time. If we drift into blame, I'll point at it. If you notice it before I do, point at it yourself. That's allowed and encouraged. Let's begin."

Proceed to the content portion of the retrospective.
