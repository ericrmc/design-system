“Before we begin, I want to set the tone for this retrospective.

The purpose of this conversation is learning, not blame. We are here to understand what happened, what made sense at the time, what got in the way, and what we want to change going forward. We are not here to decide who to fault.

Everyone in this room worked inside a system: deadlines, information gaps, handoffs, assumptions, tools, priorities, and constraints. When something went wrong, it usually made sense to someone in the moment. Our job today is to make that visible. We will talk about decisions, breakdowns, misses, and failures, but we will do that without attaching shame to individuals.

A few agreements for this discussion:
We speak from our own experience.
We describe impact clearly, without exaggeration.
We stay curious longer than feels natural.
We challenge process, conditions, and patterns before we judge people.
We assume people acted with the intent to do good work, even when the result was poor.
And if something feels sensitive, we can name it directly and handle it carefully.

It is completely acceptable to say, ‘I missed something,’ ‘I was overloaded,’ ‘I didn’t ask for help,’ or ‘I didn’t understand the risk.’ Those are useful data points, not confessions. Naming them helps us improve the system around the work.

If you disagree with someone, aim to add information, not win a case. If you notice yourself getting defensive, that is okay; just slow down and return to curiosity. If you are quieter by default, I will make space for you. If you speak often, help create room for others.

The standard for today is honesty, respect, and usefulness. We are here to leave with better shared understanding and better next steps.

With that in mind, let’s start with one thing that felt harder than it should have been.”
