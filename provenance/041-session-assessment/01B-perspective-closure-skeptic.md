# Perspective — Closure-skeptic

My position: **OI-004 should remain at state 3 after Session 041.** The retrospective should be produced, but state 3→4 should be deferred pending a genuinely cross-family data point or explicit kernel acknowledgement that closure is taken on a GPT-only record.

## Q1 — Retrospective adequacy

The §3 table is factually tight: 15 deliberations, C4-1 uniformly ✓, C4-3 ✓ from S011, ratio 12:9. Conditions (i) and (iv) are substantively met.

Two features of the table do load-bearing work for closure that its *form* does not surface:

- C4-2 is `unknown` in every single row. `unknown` is itself signal per the unknown-field rule, but a record where "no Claude-derived training dependency" is not affirmatively established for *any* of 15 rows doesn't demonstrate cross-lineage contact; it demonstrates contact with participants whose overlap with Claude is undetermined. That is a weaker thing. [synth]
- The Outsider identity column shows OpenAI/GPT-family for all 15 rows. There is zero participation from any other non-Anthropic lineage (no Gemini, no open-weights, no human-branch).

The table is sufficient to *produce* the retrospective and pass check 18, but insufficient to support closure without explicit acknowledgement that closure is on a GPT-family-only record with `unknown` C4-2 uniformly. The P4 Assertion and Tier 2 Q8 should state this narrowing bluntly.

## Q2 — Condition (iii), the contradiction-prevailing case

This is the binding question. Condition (iii) says the non-Claude participant's position must have **contradicted** Claude-perspective consensus AND the synthesis must have adopted the non-Claude position. Examining each candidate:

**S017 H4** [provenance/017-oi017-reframing-deliberation/01-deliberation.md, §2.6]. Claude perspectives were a three-way *spread* (H1/H2/H3), not a consensus. H4 rejects the either/or framing. Synthesis adopted H4. Strongest candidate, but ambiguous against condition (iii) as written: no Claude **consensus** for H4 to contradict; a three-way split. H4 contradicts a shared *frame*, not a shared *position*. Strict reading not met; looser reading (contradicted the shared either/or frame) met.

**S014 three-cell protocol** [provenance/014-oi016-resolution/01-deliberation.md, §100; §314]. Claude spread: Architect within-session, Operationalist hybrid, Skeptic hybrid. Outsider proposed sealed three-cell cross-session. No Claude perspective proposed the Curation/Produce/Validation separation-of-duties *structure* (§314: "none produced the curation/produce/validation trinity as a primary structural distinction"). Three Claude perspectives all operated within a "within-session vs hand-off" frame; Outsider replaced it; synthesis adopted the replacement. [synth] S014 is a genuine contradiction-prevailing data point.

**S021 two-branch criterion-4** [provenance/021-oi004-criterion4-articulation/01-deliberation.md, §153]. Claude perspectives framed criterion-4 within a single definitional framework. Outsider bifurcated model-provenance vs human-selection-independence. Synthesis adopted the bifurcation. Closer to **frame-completion** than **contradiction** — no Claude perspective affirmatively *rejected* a two-branch treatment; they just didn't produce it. Counting absence-of-contradiction as contradiction is the laundering pattern Session 021 R8 was designed to catch. S021 is **augmentation, not contradiction**.

**S036 workspace-identity-file class** [provenance/036-session-assessment/01-deliberation.md, §218; §226]. Reviser proposed Direction 2 (structural-signature); Synthesiser proposed structural-primary + MODE.md override; Outsider proposed MODE.md-authoritative with category-error critique. Synthesis landed on a hybrid (MODE.md primary + structural fallback) — not pure-Outsider Direction 1 (§10.4-M4 explicitly: "Outsider pure Direction 1 rejected in favour of hybrid"). The non-Claude position *partially* prevailed. However the workspace-identity-file class distinction [§226] *was* fully adopted. Split: class distinction is contradiction-prevailing; dispatch structure is hybrid-compromise. [synth]

**My Q2 answer**: S014 clearly meets condition (iii). S036's workspace-identity-file class distinction meets it. S017 meets it under a looser reading. S021 does not. The retrospective can honestly cite S014 as decisive; citing S017 or S021 should flag the ambiguity. If the retrospective relies on S021 alone, WX-21-2 has not been cleanly discharged.

## Q3 — Close now vs continue state 3

Procedurally, condition (iii) is met via S014. But closure should be deferred on a different ground: **the record is GPT-family only, C4-2 uniformly unknown, synthesis single-Claude**. Closing on this record without kernel-level acknowledgement that closure rests on single-non-Claude-family contact leaves the methodology vulnerable to the Session 021 Skeptic §5.1 strict-enumeration concern.

**My proposal**: produce the retrospective now; P4 Assertion cites S014 as decisive; Tier 2 Q8 substantively answers the GPT-only narrowing; state 3→4 deferred one session, contingent on either (a) a Session 042 deliberation with a non-GPT-family Outsider, or (b) a kernel amendment documenting that OI-004 closes on GPT-family-only contact with explicit forward-discipline. Closure without either is over-fitted to the one lineage we happen to have operational access to.

## Q4 — §5.2 + §5.3 activation disposition

Both materially activated. Activation is a *trigger to revisit*, not automatic vindication. Session 021 R4 adopted keep-open on three grounds; at Session 041 the record tests them:

- **Cross-family threshold**: still only one non-Claude family exercised. Not vindicated against; original Skeptic concern holds.
- **Convergence-by-mechanism**: partially testable. If the retrospective lands on S014, the mechanism has produced at least one genuine cross-lineage frame-contradiction data point — modest vindication.
- **Asymmetry argument**: unchanged. Still asymmetric to close prematurely.

**My position**: §5.2 (close-on-articulation) is NOT vindicated — 20 sessions of closure-retrospective discipline produced real analytical work that immediate-close would have foreclosed. §5.3 ("indefinitely movable finish line") is partially vindicated in *procedure* (20 sessions is long) but not in *substance* (the finish line is being defined now, not moved). Continued preservation of both if closure deferred per Q3; §5.3 vindicated and §5.2 continued-preserved if closure proceeds.

## Q5 — Self-application risk

Session 041 is itself required-trigger; its Outsider is GPT-5.4 (same lineage as all 15 prior rows). If Session 041 counts as the 16th data point, closure is taken on a record including the session closing it — circular. If Session 041 does not count, the terminal deliberation is strangely excluded from the basis. This is a **designed closeable-gate**, but the gate is only non-circular if Session 041's Outsider contributes *new* information the pre-041 record did not settle. If the Outsider merely ratifies §3, the gate is cosmetic.

Session 021 R4's three grounds hold up *partially* — cross-family threshold NOT discharged; other two partially tested. R4 is partially vindicated with one ground still live.

## Q6 — Forward implications if OI-004 closes

If closed: d023_4 triggers should be retired (advancing a closed OI is no longer a required-trigger condition), but clause 4 of §When Non-Claude Participation Is Required should remain for kernel/spec changes. Cross-model discipline without a "watchable OI" anchor is at risk — closure should be contingent on adopting a standing watchpoint tracking cumulative non-Claude-family diversity, so the discipline doesn't degrade once OI-004 is closed. Without such a watchpoint, closure is load-shedding discipline rather than graduating it.
