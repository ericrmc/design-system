## Case-by-case audit

### Case S017 — H4 layered model

1. **Claude-consensus characterisation**. No single consensus — the three Claude perspectives split H1/H2/H3 three ways: Architect H2 (full reframe), Operationalist H3 (partial), Skeptic H1 (no reframe) `[017/01-deliberation.md §1]`. What they shared was the either/or framing of the brief's hypothesis set.
2. **Outsider contribution**. The Outsider explicitly rejected the H1/H2/H3 framing and proposed a fourth: *"My position is a fourth hypothesis: make the stack explicit as methodology -> engine -> application. Selvedge remains the methodology; the Selvedge engine is the current loadable implementation; each run against self-development or an external problem is an application of that engine."* `[017/01-deliberation.md §2.6]`. The Outsider also challenged the frame itself as *"entangling two different decisions that should be separated: what Selvedge is, and what must be loaded to run it"* `[017/01-deliberation.md §3(5)]`.
3. **Contradiction vs augmentation vs completion**. **Frame-replacement**, stronger than contradiction-of-a-consensus. Strictly, no Claude consensus existed to contradict (three-way split); but the Outsider rejected the either/or itself and supplied a layered alternative none of the three Claude perspectives produced. Synthesis calls this *"the central cross-model contribution"* `[017/01-deliberation.md §2.6]`.
4. **Synthesis adoption**. Explicit. §6 recommendation reads *"Adopt H4 (Outsider-originated layered model)"* `[017/01-deliberation.md §6]`. The Outsider's exact language is adopted verbatim for identity.md v2 `[017/01-deliberation.md §2.4, §6(1)]`. The decision D-074 adopted H4 per synthesis; no Claude perspective produced H4.
5. **Grade: 3 (strong).** Frame-replacement, Outsider-cited-as-source, synthesis adopts Outsider language verbatim.

### Case S014 — three-cell protocol

1. **Claude-consensus characterisation**. No unified consensus on Q3 validation shape: Architect pure within-session (4 internal roles); Operationalist exercise-scoped hand-off across sessions; Skeptic within-session-then-hand-off hybrid `[014/01-deliberation.md Q3]`. All three **fail to produce a Curation-Produce-Validation separation of duties at the primary-structural level** — they organise around sessions and agent-roles instead.
2. **Outsider contribution**. Direct quote from synthesis: *"A sealed three-cell protocol combining hand-off across sessions with multi-agent judging. Cell 1: Curation. Cell 2: Produce. Cell 3: Validation."* `[014/01-deliberation.md Q3, 01d]`. The synthesis explicitly attributes the structural framing to the Outsider: *"the decisive structural framing. Separation of duties between Curation Cell... Produce Cell... Validation Cell... this separation is a cross-model-originated structure; no Claude perspective produced the three-cell framing at this level"* `[014/01-deliberation.md Q3]`.
3. **Contradiction vs augmentation vs completion**. **Substantive augmentation.** The three Claude shapes are not mutually exclusive with three-cell — they fit inside Cell 2 or across Cells 2–3 — but the separation-of-duties primary framing is absent from all three, and the synthesis adopts the Outsider's framing as the primary organiser. Clears the *"contradicts (or substantively augments)"* bar `[MAD v4 §Closure iii per brief §2]`.
4. **Synthesis adoption**. Explicit: *"Synthesis Q3 position: adopt the Outsider-originated three-cell protocol"* `[014/01-deliberation.md Q3]`. §Cross-model contributions §1 names it as the first enumerated Outsider-originated contribution: *"Sealed three-cell protocol (Curation + Produce + Validation) as the Q3 adopted shape"* `[014/01-deliberation.md cross-model §1]`.
5. **Grade: 3 (strong).** Outsider-cited-as-source for the primary structural framing; synthesis explicitly adopts and attributes; Claude perspectives each produced weaker alternatives.

The Outsider position summary `[021/01-deliberation.md §1]` confirms this as the Outsider's Q1 frame.
